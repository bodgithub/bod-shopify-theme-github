# Ad → Landing Page Context
# READ before building any landing page or paid-traffic page. This governs what a page must SAY
# so it continues the ad that sent the visitor. (Media-buying mechanics are out of scope here.)

## Core positioning the page must carry
Metabolism-first approach to hormone health. We reframe symptoms (cravings, mood dips, irregular
cycles, fatigue) as connected metabolic signals — not character flaws, not "just hormones." Removes
shame and self-blame. Opens the audience beyond diagnosed PCOS to any woman with metabolic dysfunction.

## Core ad angles / hooks we run (continue these on the page)
Lead a page with the SAME thread the ad opened. Proven and active angles:
- Dismissed-by-doctors: "You deserve more than 'everything looks normal.'"
- Symptoms are connected/metabolic: "Your symptoms were never random. They were connected."
  / "PCOS symptoms aren't just hormonal. They're metabolic."
- Post-pill: "Stopped the pill and still waiting for your cycle?"
- Supplement fatigue / metabolic root: "Before you try another supplement…"
- Mood reframe: "Mood dips before your period aren't a character flaw."
- Hero brand line (problem-aware, proven): "You were never broken." (Still in active use — NOT retired.)
- Education stats used as hooks (must cite study + disclaimer): "80% of women with PCOS have insulin
  resistance," "2 in 5 women in the US have insulin resistance," "85% of women saw their cycles return."
  Use the weight/BMI and infertility stats with care — avoid body-shaming or fear framing.

## MESSAGE-MATCH RULE (non-negotiable)
A landing page's hook/headline MUST continue the promise of the ad that sent the visitor. If the ad
said "Stopped the pill?", the page opens on post-pill, not generic PCOS. Never open a different
conversation than the ad started. We run five problem lenses, each with its own PDP view — match the page to the lens:
PCOS/PMOS · Hormones · Fertility · Metabolism · Mood.

## Traffic temperature — how pages differ
- COLD / problem-aware (she doesn't know inositol or Bodology exists):
  Lead with the hook + the felt problem. Educate (name the metabolic root, the mechanism) BEFORE
  the product. Build trust, then introduce the product low in the page. Don't open on price.
- WARM / retargeting (knows us, hasn't bought):
  Go faster to offer + proof. Lead with proof/credibility and differentiation, address objections
  (does it work, which brand, price/trust), surface the offer early.

## Platform feel (must carry to the page)
- Paid: Meta is the primary paid channel. TikTok is used for organic/short video and as a creative

- Creative tone: raw, native, UGC-style, mobile-first. Macro/close-up imagery of body/skin, warm and

  not a polished corporate landing page.

## AD-COMPLIANCE (page copy must be ad-safe, not just FDA-safe)
Any claim that would get an ad disapproved on Meta/TikTok is also banned on the page. In particular:
- No drug comparisons (Metformin/SSRIs) — disapproval risk AND off-brand.
- No "treat/cure/reverse/prevent [disease]," no guaranteed outcomes, no diagnosing the reader,
  no before/after extremes, no "miracle."
- No personal-attribute targeting language ("are you struggling with PCOS?") framed as assumed fact.
Keep page copy within compliance.md. Flag borderline lines for review.
