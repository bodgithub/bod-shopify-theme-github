# Compliance — Structure/Function vs. Disease Claims
# This is a dietary supplement. Every external line must pass this file. When unsure, FLAG, don't ship.

## The line
We can say the product SUPPORTS normal structures/functions of the body. We CANNOT say it
diagnoses, treats, cures, prevents, reverses, or manages any disease or names a disease as a target.

## ALLOWED — structure/function language (always paired with the FDA disclaimer)
- "Supports hormonal balance*"
- "Supports menstrual cycle regularity*"
- "Supports healthy glucose metabolism*"
- "Supports healthy blood sugar levels already in the normal range*"
- "Helps maintain healthy blood sugar metabolism after eating*"
- "Supports healthy insulin function*"
- "Supports appetite regulation / helps manage cravings*"
- "Supports healthy body composition, in combination with diet and exercise*"
- "Supports mood and stress resilience*"
- "Clinically studied" / "backed by published research" (when citing real studies)
- Framing words: "supports," "helps support," "designed to support," "may support"

## BANNED — disease claims & non-compliant phrasing
- Anything that says treat / cure / prevent / reverse / manage + a disease, including:
  treat PCOS, manage PCOS, reverse insulin resistance, prevent diabetes, treat PMDD, cure infertility.
- "Lowers blood sugar" / "lowers your blood sugar" (implies it acts on a disease state).
- Any drug comparison: "as effective as Metformin," "comparable to / replaces SSRIs/antidepressants,"
  "alternative to [drug]," "stop taking your medication."
  ⚠️ These claims CURRENTLY appear in the Brand Ethos doc and one ad variant — they must be scrubbed.
  Inositol is framed as clinical optionality, never positioned against a drug.
- Guarantees of outcome: "guaranteed results," "will fix your hormones," "cures your cravings."
- "Miracle," "only solution," "the only thing you need," before/after extremes, claims without explanation.
- Diagnosing the reader ("you have insulin resistance"). Use "may be a sign of" / "can be linked to."

## Borderline — FLAG for Jasmin, do not ship without sign-off
- "PCOS symptom management" and "weight loss" — these appear on the internal doctor-approved list
  but sit close to disease/outcome claims. Flag any on-page use for review.
- Specific outcome stats ("85% of women saw their cycles return," "fat loss by 35%") — only use with
  the cited study and the disclaimer; flag for review.

## Rules
1. Every claim line carries the FDA disclaimer (verbatim from product-facts.md).
2. "Supports" / "may support," never "guarantees."
3. No disease as a target or outcome.
4. FLAG any borderline line for my review rather than shipping it. When unsure, it's borderline.
