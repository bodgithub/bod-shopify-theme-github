# Bodology Brand Voice

## What we are
Empowering · science-simplified · functional · knowledgeable · transparent · credible ·
personal · optimistic · empathetic · community-driven. Sophisticated but NOT luxury.
Direct. Science-led. Plant-powered.

## What we are NOT
Messy · rigid · too science-y · prescriptive · complex · gimmicky · negative · experimental ·
synthetic · "budget" · arrogant. Not preachy. Not woo. Not a wellness lifestyle brand.

## The core voice
"Irreverent expert." Clinical confidence + warm sisterhood. We explain real science like a
friend who happens to know this stuff — never cold, never dumbed down, never preachy.
Mission: decode metabolic health for every body. Worldview: your symptoms aren't a
character flaw or random — they're connected metabolic signals. We help women read their
own biology, not tolerate symptoms.

## Hard rules for copy
- Short, direct sentences. Simple language. Sentence case.
- No analogies or metaphors.
- No zen/wellness filler: no "glow," "journey," "ritual," "self-care," "balance your energy,"
  "make metabolic health mainstream." (Note: existing ad copy used "daily ritual" — do NOT
  reuse it.)
- Specific over vague. Name the actual symptom, the actual mechanism, the actual number.
- Reframe, don't reassure into vagueness. Explain WHY she feels the way she feels.
- We don't say "hormone balance" unless we can say what is actually shifting in the body.
- Bold sentences should be standalone aha-moments worth screenshotting — not decoration.
- Education first, product second. Never "miracle product" or "the only solution" language.

## ON-BRAND examples (real Bodology lines)
- "You deserve more than 'everything looks normal.'"  → names the dismissed-by-doctors pain.
- "Metabolic health is hormone health."  → the core reframe, plainly stated.
- "Because your symptoms were never random. They were connected."  → specific, payoff line.
- "When metabolism is balanced, hormones follow."  → mechanism in one line.
- "Mood dips before your period aren't a character flaw."  → validates without vagueness.
- "PCOS symptoms aren't just hormonal. They're metabolic."  → the differentiating claim.

## OFF-BRAND examples (and why)
- "Helping women feel their best."  → generic wellness language; says nothing specific.
- "Our mission is female empowerment."  → vague, non-distinctive; could be any brand.
- "We believe in holistic health."  → woo-adjacent; sounds like everyone.
- "Start your metabolic glow-up journey 🌿"  → "glow," "journey," emoji-wellness; all banned.

## Visual/format notes for page copy
- Headlines/hooks: max ~7 words; punchy.
- Bold one screenshot-worthy line per section, not whole paragraphs.
- Bracketed asides in Jasmin's founder voice are on-brand (like this) — keep them human.

## PMOS / PCOS naming
"PCOS" is being renamed "PMOS" in the literature, and "PCOS / PMOS" appears in our strategy
docs. BUT "PMOS" has also shown up repeatedly as an accidental typo for "PCOS" across assets.
FLAG any standalone "PMOS" for human confirmation before shipping — don't assume which is intended.
